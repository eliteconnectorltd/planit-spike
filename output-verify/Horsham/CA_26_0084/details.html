Received

We have received the application and are checking things such as:

  • Has the application form been completed correctly?
  • Have all the required plans and documents been submitted? Are they correct?
  • Has the right fee is paid?

Applications cannot be viewed here during this phase.

Validated

The application has now passed its initial checks. Now we:

  • allocate it to a planning officer
  • send an acknowledgement letter to the applicant.

Consultation

We usually tell people about the application and invite comments (some applications don't need this). The way we do this depends on the type of application, but we may:

  • Put up site notices
  • Advertise in local newspapers
  • Write to neighbours
  • Write to statutory bodies and specialist organisations

By law, we have to allow time for people to send us their comments. For most applications, this is 21 days - the deadline is shown on the Important Dates page on this website. In practice, we accept comments after this date (up until we issue the final decision or the application is put on an agenda to go to planning committee).

Comments are an important part of the application process. We consider the points raised, but not the number of comments for or against. For more detail about what sorts of points we can consider, see https://www.horsham.gov.uk/planning/planning-applications/view-and-comment-on-planning-applications.

Recommendation and/or Committee

  • We check the application against local policies and other considerations (such as appearance and noise)
  • We consider the comments made by local people and statutory bodies
  • We may visit the site
  • We may ask the applicant for more information

The final decision may be made by the planning officer, or by the Planning Committee if the application is:

  • for a large development
  • contentious
  • made by a councillor or council employee
  • involves legal obligations

If an application goes to Planning Committee, we write to everyone who commented about 10 days before the meeting. The letter explains how you can register to speak at the meeting.

​

Decided

  • We send a decision notice to the applicant . We can either permit or refuse the application. If we permit it, there are usually conditions which the applicant must follow.
  • You can read the decision notice and the officer's report on this website.
  • Only the applicant can appeal the decision. They usually have 6 months to do this. Other people cannot appeal, but can request a judicial review if we haven't followed the legal process correctly.
